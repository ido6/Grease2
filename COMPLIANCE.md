# Amendment 13 Compliance Documentation

## Overview

This document outlines the compliance measures implemented for Israel's Privacy Protection Law Amendment 13 (תיקון 13 לחוק הגנת הפרטיות), effective August 14, 2025.

## Data Controller Information

- **Company Name:** Grease Shoes
- **Location:** Israel
- **Contact:** Available through the contact form on the website

## Data Processing Activities

### Types of Personal Data Collected

1. **Personal Information Provided by Users:**
   - Full name
   - Email address
   - Phone number (optional)
   - Address (if relevant for orders)
   - Messages and other information shared via contact forms

2. **Technical Information Collected Automatically:**
   - IP address
   - Browser type and operating system
   - Pages visited
   - Time and date of visit
   - Referral source

### Purposes of Data Processing

1. **Service Provision:** Responding to inquiries and providing requested services
2. **User Experience:** Improving website navigation and functionality
3. **Important Updates:** Sending essential messages related to requested services
4. **Marketing (with explicit consent):** Sending updates about new products, promotions, and special offers
5. **Statistical Analysis:** Analyzing usage patterns to improve the website and services
6. **Security and Legal Compliance:** Protecting the website from fraud, enforcing terms of use, and complying with legal obligations

### Legal Basis for Processing

- **Consent:** For marketing communications and non-essential cookies
- **Legitimate Interest:** For essential website functionality and security
- **Legal Obligation:** For compliance with applicable laws and regulations

## Data Sharing and Processors

### Third-Party Service Providers

1. **Hosting Providers:** Companies hosting the website on their servers
2. **Analytics Services:** Tools for analyzing user behavior (only with user consent for analytics cookies)
3. **Email Services:** Providers for sending email messages

All service providers are contractually obligated to maintain data confidentiality and use data only for specified purposes.

### Cross-Border Data Transfers

Some service providers may be located outside of Israel. When transferring personal data outside of Israel, we ensure that appropriate data protection agreements are in place or that the receiving country is on Israel's list of countries with adequate protection levels.

## Cookie Usage

### Cookie Categories

1. **Essential Cookies:** Required for website operation (no consent required)
   - Language preferences
   - Security features
   - Basic website functions

2. **Analytics Cookies:** Require explicit consent
   - Visitor counts
   - Time spent on site
   - Navigation patterns

3. **Marketing Cookies:** Require explicit consent
   - Personalized advertising
   - Campaign effectiveness measurement

## User Rights Implementation

### Right of Access
Users can request a copy of all personal data we hold about them through the [Data Request Form](/data-request).

### Right to Rectification
Users can request correction or update of inaccurate or outdated information.

### Right to Erasure
Users can request deletion of their personal data, subject to legal limitations.

### Right to Object
Users can object to processing of their data for marketing purposes.

### Right to Withdraw Consent
Users can withdraw consent at any time through [Privacy Settings](/privacy-settings).

## Consent Management

### Consent Mechanisms

1. **Cookie Consent Banner:** Appears on first visit, allows granular consent options
2. **Contact Form Consent:** Required checkbox with full legal text
3. **Privacy Settings Page:** Allows users to manage and withdraw consent

### Consent Logging

All consents are logged with:
- Timestamp
- Consent type
- Consent status (granted/denied)
- User identifier (if available)
- User agent
- Version of consent text/policy

Consent logs are stored in localStorage and should be backed up to a server database for compliance purposes.

## Data Retention

### Retention Periods

1. **Contact Form Data:** Up to 3 years from the date of inquiry, unless deletion is requested earlier
2. **Technical Data (IP addresses, logs):** Up to 2 years, in accordance with data security regulations
3. **Marketing Data (with consent):** Until consent is withdrawn or up to 5 years from last collection, whichever is earlier
4. **Legally Required Data:** According to legal retention requirements (e.g., financial records, tax records)

## Security Measures

### Technical Safeguards

1. **Encryption:** Data encrypted in transit (HTTPS)
2. **Access Controls:** Limited access to personal data only to employees who need it
3. **Authentication:** Strong authentication systems
4. **Monitoring:** Security monitoring and periodic testing
5. **Backups:** Secure backups

### Organizational Safeguards

1. **Staff Training:** Privacy and security training for staff
2. **Privacy by Design:** Privacy considerations in new projects
3. **Incident Response:** Documented procedures for handling data breaches

## Data Breach Procedures

In the event of a significant data security breach that affects personal data:

1. **Notification to PPA:** Report to the Privacy Protection Authority as required by law
2. **User Notification:** Notify affected users if the breach may endanger their rights or freedoms
3. **Documentation:** Document the breach, response actions, and remediation measures

## Vendor Management

### Due Diligence

Before onboarding vendors who process personal data:
- Conduct privacy and security due diligence
- Review vendor's data protection practices
- Verify compliance with applicable laws

### Data Processing Agreements (DPAs)

All vendors processing personal data must sign DPAs that include:
- Data confidentiality obligations
- Purpose limitation
- Security requirements
- Breach notification obligations
- Assistance with user rights requests

### Ongoing Oversight

- Monitor vendor compliance
- Request annual security compliance reports
- Review and update agreements as needed

## Privacy Protection Officer (PPO)

Currently, the company is not required to appoint a Privacy Protection Officer under the law. However, privacy compliance is managed internally, and a PPO will be appointed if the company meets the legal thresholds (e.g., large-scale processing of sensitive data, systematic monitoring).

## Compliance Monitoring

### Regular Reviews

- Quarterly review of privacy practices
- Annual security audits
- Regular updates to privacy policy and procedures
- Staff training updates

### Documentation

- Maintain records of processing activities
- Document consent logs
- Keep security audit reports
- Document data breach incidents and responses
